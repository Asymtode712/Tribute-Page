Nambi Narayanan


The Forgotten Hero of ISRO

Nambi Narayanan is an Indian aerospace scientist who worked for the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). As a senior official at the ISRO, he was briefly in charge of the cryogenics division. He was awarded the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award, in March 2019. In 1994, he was arrested on trumped up charges of espionage. The charges against him were found to be baseless by the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) in April 1996. As a result, the Supreme Court of India dismissed all charges against him and prohibited the Government of Kerala from continuing its investigation on technical grounds. In 2018, a Supreme Court bench headed by then Chief Justice Dipak Misra, awarded Narayanan a compensation of ₹50 lakh (equivalent to ₹67 lakh or US$84,000 in 2023).Additionally, the Government of Kerala then awarded him further compensation to the tune of ₹1.3 crore (equivalent to ₹1.7 crore or US$220,000 in 2023) in 2019. The film Rocketry: The Nambi Effect, based on his life, starring and directed by R. Madhavan, was released in July 2022.

Timeline

1941

Nambi Narayanan is born on December 12 in Nagercoil, a town in the erstwhile Princely state of Travancore, to Tamil Hindu parents. His early life in Nagercoil is marked by the influence of his parents and the pursuit of education.

1966

Narayanan starts his career at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) as a technical assistant at the Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station. This marks the beginning of his journey in the field of aerospace and rocketry.

1969

He is sent to Princeton University for a master's program in chemical rocket propulsion, sponsored by the Government of India. At Princeton, he acquires expertise in liquid propulsion, a critical area for Indian rocketry at the time, which heavily relied on solid propellants.

1994

In November, Nambi Narayanan is arrested on charges of alleged espionage. He is accused of transferring drawings and documents of rocket engines to two Maldivian women suspected to be spies. This event becomes a turning point in his life as he faces false accusations and legal proceedings.

1996

In April, the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) finds the charges against Nambi Narayanan to be baseless, stating there was no espionage and that testimonies of suspects were coerced through torture. This marks the exoneration of Narayanan from the false charges.

1998

The Kerala government revokes CBI's permission to investigate the case, but a Supreme Court bench orders the government to pay ₹1 lakh to each of the accused, including Narayanan. This legal battle reveals flaws in the investigation and leads to compensation for those wrongfully accused.

1999

The National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) passes strictures against the Kerala government for damaging Narayanan's career in space research and subjecting him and his family to physical and mental torture. This acknowledgment of the human rights violations adds further weight to Narayanan's case.

2001

The NHRC orders the Government of Kerala to pay Nambi Narayanan a compensation of ₹1 crore. This significant compensation acknowledges the injustices he endured throughout the ordeal.

2018

The Supreme Court appoints a panel to probe Narayanan's "harrowing" arrest and alleged torture, awarding him ₹50 lakh in compensation for the "mental cruelty" he endured. This marks another step toward seeking justice for the trauma he experienced.

2019

Nambi Narayanan is awarded the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award. This recognition is a testament to his contributions to the field of space research and his perseverance in the face of adversity.

2021

The Kerala government settles the case filed by Narayanan by agreeing to a payment of ₹1.3 crore. This settlement provides some closure to the long-standing legal battle and offers financial redress to Narayanan.

2022

The film "Rocketry: The Nambi Effect," based on Nambi Narayanan's life, is released, starring and directed by R. Madhavan. The film serves as a platform to bring his story to a wider audience and shed light on his experiences and contributions.
